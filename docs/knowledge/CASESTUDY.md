## 1. Premise
A gamified career-discovery experience for RoboticsCareer.org that turns a vague "maybe robotics" into a specific, personal "here are real roles I could grow into." Replaces three weak existing tools with one focused flow. *(Full why: `CONTEXT_BRIEF.md`.)*

## 2. The problem (research → design)
- The barrier is **lack of specificity**, not lack of interest; a large "maybe" group can't picture the work.
- Anchored in Jessica Hammer's *epistemic games* — let people **try on** a professional identity.
- *(Evidence: `CONTEXT_BRIEF.md`.)*

## 3. The approach — an agentic design practice
What's distinctive isn't just the artifact; it's **how it was built**: a small, owned agent + design harness (context · skills · orchestration · evaluation · knowledge) that keeps a multi-week, multi-session build coherent and reviewable.
- Bespoke harness, tuned not cargo-culted — see D-007, L-002.
- Evaluation made checkable: design rubrics + a screenshot-grading `design-reviewer` subagent.
- Knowledge compounds in-repo so it survives context resets and onboards teammates / the ARM handoff.
- *(Setup narrative: `sessions/2026-05-29-docs-reconciliation-and-harness.md`.)*
